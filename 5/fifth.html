Normalized Comparison of Life Expectancy, GDP, and Population Across Major Economies

Type: Grouped Bar Chart

This grouped bar chart offers a normalized comparison of three essential metrics—Life Expectancy, GDP per capita, and Population—across five significant economies: the United States, China, India, Japan, and Germany. Each bar group represents a country, with individual bars displaying normalized values for each metric. Normalization ensures that the differences in scale between these metrics are accounted for, enabling a clearer visual comparison. This chart provides a comprehensive overview of how these countries compare in terms of health outcomes, economic performance, and demographic size, facilitating a deeper understanding of their relative standings.
